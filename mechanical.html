Metal

Metal is used in many different forms in FRC but team 2702 uses 6061 Alloy Aluminum and a small amount of Steel. Steel is much more structuralully solid, but will have a much larger affect on the robot weight.

Fasteners

A fastener is anything used to hold a part together. This could be a screw, rivit, nut, nylock, and many more. They vary depending on the use, and come in mnay different sizes.

Drive Motors

A drive motor is any motor that has enough power to move a robot when attatched to the proper gearbox. Main ones include CIM's, which are the classic. Neo's were released in 2019 with the benifit of being smaller, and Falcon's (2020) have more power but draw more current.

Misc. Motors

Other motors usually control subsystems on the robot and can vary widely. Main ones used in FRC include 775 Pro's, Bag Motors, 9015 motors, and many others.

CAD

CAD is used to plan out mechanical parts, and is usually used as a blueprint of the robot. It can be used to create STL files which can then be printed or lasercut using CNC machines.

Tools

There are many tools used for the manufacturing process, including power tools like drill presses, mills, and bandsaws. There are also tools like screwdrives and wrenches used for fasteners.
